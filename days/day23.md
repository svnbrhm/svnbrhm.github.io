<details open>
  <summary><strong>Breakfast & Exploration at Tsukiji Outer Market (築地場外市場) (Approx. 7:15 AM - 9:15 AM)</strong></summary>
  <div class="details-content-wrapper">
    <h4>Vibe</h4>
    <p>A bustling collection of retail shops, food stalls, and small restaurants adjacent to the former wholesale fish market. Famous for fresh seafood, produce, kitchenware, and incredible street food. Ideal for a vibrant breakfast experience.</p>
    <h4>Activity</h4>
    <ul>
      <li><i class="fas fa-utensils fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Breakfast:</span> <span class="detail-value">Wander the stalls and eateries. Try fresh sushi, sashimi bowls (kaisendon), grilled seafood skewers, tamagoyaki (rolled omelette), fruit, mochi, and other local specialties.</span></li>
      <li><i class="fas fa-shopping-bag fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Explore:</span> <span class="detail-value">Browse shops selling dried goods, pickles, tea, knives, and ceramics.</span></li>
    </ul>
    <h4>Popular Breakfast Spots/Items (Examples):</h4>
    <ul>
      <li><i class="fas fa-concierge-bell fa-fw"></i> **Kitsuneya (きつねや):** Famous for *horumon-don* (beef offal stew over rice). Opens very early (~6:30 AM). Cash only. Expect queues. <a href="https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=APA+Hotel+Asakusa+Kuramaekita%2C+Tokyo&destination=Ichiran+Asakusa+Rokku%2C+Tokyo&travelmode=walking2" target="_blank">Map</a></li>
      <li><i class="fas fa-fish fa-fw"></i> **Sushi Zanmai / Sushidai Honkan (Outer Market Branches):** Several sushi restaurants open early for breakfast. Quality varies, Sushidai can have very long waits.</li>
      <li><i class="fas fa-egg fa-fw"></i> **Tamagoyaki Shops:** Multiple stalls like Yamacho, Tsukiji Shoro, Marutake sell freshly made tamagoyaki on sticks or in blocks.</li>
      <li><i class="fas fa-bread-slice fa-fw"></i> **Street Food Stalls:** Grilled scallops, uni (sea urchin), oysters, anago (sea eel), mochi, fruit.</li>
    </ul>
    <h4>Details</h4>
    <ul>
      <li><i class="far fa-clock fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Hours:</span> <span class="detail-value">Many shops open by 6:00-7:00 AM and close by 1:00-2:00 PM. Friday should be fully operational.</span></li>
      <li><i class="far fa-credit-card fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Payment:</span> <span class="detail-value">**Bring Cash.** While some larger restaurants might accept cards, many stalls and smaller shops are cash-only.</span></li>
      <li><i class="fas fa-map-marker-alt fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">General Area Map:</span> <span class="detail-value"><a href="https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=APA+Hotel+Asakusa+Kuramaekita%2C+Tokyo&destination=Ichiran+Asakusa+Rokku%2C+Tokyo&travelmode=walking3" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">View Tsukiji Outer Market</a></span></li>
    </ul>

<h3 id="practical-info">Practical Information</h3>

<h4>About Daikanyama & Ebisu Areas</h4>
<p>Daikanyama and Ebisu are upscale, relaxed neighborhoods that offer a more sophisticated side of Tokyo. They're popular with fashion-conscious locals and are often compared to areas like Brooklyn in New York. While adjacent to bustling Shibuya, these areas have a noticeably different pace and atmosphere.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Daikanyama:</strong> Known for high-end boutiques, stylish cafes, and the architectural marvel of T-Site. The area has a relaxed, sophisticated vibe with tree-lined streets.</li>
  <li><strong>Ebisu:</strong> Named after the beer once brewed here, Ebisu offers excellent dining options, cultural facilities at Yebisu Garden Place, and a slightly more business-oriented atmosphere.</li>
  <li><strong>Nakameguro:</strong> Just one station away, this trendy area is famous for its canal lined with cherry trees and hip independent shops and cafes.</li>
</ul>

<h4>Shopping Tips</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Most boutiques in Daikanyama open around 11:00 AM, later than department stores</li>
  <li>Many shops offer tax-free shopping for foreign visitors (bring your passport)</li>
  <li>Prices tend to be higher than in more mainstream shopping areas, reflecting the curated, designer focus</li>
  <li>Staff in most Daikanyama/Ebisu shops speak some English</li>
  <li>Credit cards are widely accepted in these upscale areas</li>
</ul>

<h4>Dining Culture</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Lunch Sets:</strong> Many restaurants offer excellent value lunch sets (teishoku) that are significantly cheaper than dinner prices</li>
  <li><strong>Izakaya Etiquette:</strong> At places like Motsuyaki Ban, expect to order drinks first, followed by food items throughout your meal</li>
  <li><strong>Seating Charge:</strong> Some establishments have a table charge (otōshidai) of ¥300-500 per person</li>
  <li><strong>Tipping:</strong> Not expected or practiced in Japan</li>
</ul>

<h4>Weather Considerations (Late May)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Late May in Tokyo typically offers pleasant temperatures (18-25°C/64-77°F)</li>
  <li>Rain is possible as the rainy season approaches in June</li>
  <li>Many cafes and restaurants in Daikanyama have both indoor and outdoor seating</li>
  <li>If it's sunny, the outdoor seating at Log Road Daikanyama is particularly pleasant</li>
</ul>
