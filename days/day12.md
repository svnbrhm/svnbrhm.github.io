   <h4>Cultural Context</h4>
   <p>Fukuoka Castle (Fukuoka-jō) was once one of Japan's largest castles, built in the early 1600s by feudal lord Kuroda Nagamasa. Though most structures were dismantled after the Meiji Restoration, the impressive stone walls and foundations that remain give insight into the scale and strategic importance of this former military stronghold. The castle was positioned to control access to northern Kyushu and maintain power in the region.</p>
   
   <p>Ohori Park was created in 1929, incorporating part of the old castle's moat system. The name "Ohori" literally means "moat," reflecting this history. The park's design was inspired by China's famous West Lake in Hangzhou.</p>
   
   <h4>Weather Contingency</h4>
