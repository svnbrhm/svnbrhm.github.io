  <div class="details-content-wrapper">
  <h4>Activity Info</h4>
   <ul>
    <li><i class="fas fa-info-circle fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">About:</span> <span class="detail-value">Japan's most spectacular feudal castle, a UNESCO World Heritage site known as "White Heron Castle" for its elegant white appearance. Explore the complex grounds and climb the multi-story main keep (tenshukaku) via steep, narrow wooden stairs for historical insights and city views.</span></li>
    <li><i class="fas fa-stopwatch fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Duration:</span> <span class="detail-value">Allow <strong>minimum 2-3 hours</strong> for a thorough visit including grounds and keep interior.</span></li>
    <li><i class="far fa-clock fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Hours:</span> <span class="detail-value">9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (Last entry 4:00 PM).</span></li>
    <li><i class="fas fa-ticket-alt fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Price:</span> <span class="detail-value">Castle only: Approx. ¥1000 / €5.92. <strong>Combination Ticket with Kokoen Garden: Approx. ¥1050 / €6.21 (Recommended Value!)</strong>.</span></li>
    <li><i class="far fa-calendar-check fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Reservations:</span> <span class="detail-value">Not usually required. Purchase tickets at the main entrance booth (Hishi Gate).</span></li>
    <li><i class="fas fa-users fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Queues:</span> <span class="detail-value">Can be busy. Weekday mornings generally better. Short waits for keep entry possible during peak times.</span></li>
    <li><i class="fas fa-headphones-alt fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Audio Guides:</span> <span class="detail-value">Available for rent near entrance (¥500).</span></li>
    <li><i class="fas fa-map-marker-alt fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Map:</span></li>
   </ul>
   {% mapEmbed "Himeji Castle" %}
   
   <h4>Cultural & Historical Context</h4>
   <p>Himeji Castle is considered Japan's finest surviving example of prototypical Japanese castle architecture. Originally built in the 14th century and extensively expanded in the early 17th century, it miraculously survived WWII bombing raids that destroyed many other Japanese castles. Its complex defensive design features multiple baileys (walled areas), gates, and winding paths designed to confuse attackers.</p>
   
   <p>The castle's brilliant white exterior earned it the nickname "White Heron Castle" (Shirasagi-jo), as it appears to be a white bird taking flight when viewed from a distance. After a major restoration completed in 2015, the castle regained its dazzling white appearance.</p>
   
   <p>Unlike many Japanese castles that are concrete reconstructions, Himeji Castle is mostly original wooden construction, making it particularly significant. Its design represents the peak of Japanese castle development, with defensive and aesthetic considerations perfectly balanced.</p>
   
   <h4>Visit Tips</h4>
   <ul>
    <li><strong>One-Way System:</strong> The main keep follows a one-way route with narrow staircases. Be prepared for steep climbing.</li>
    <li><strong>Shoes:</strong> You'll need to remove shoes when entering the main keep, so wear socks and shoes that are easy to take on/off.</li>
    <li><strong>Photography:</strong> No tripods allowed inside buildings. Best exterior photo spots include the Sannomaru Square (main approach) and the West Bailey (Nishinomaru).</li>
    <li><strong>Accessibility:</strong> The main keep involves steep stairs and is not wheelchair accessible. However, the grounds and some outer buildings are accessible.</li>
   </ul>
   
   <h4>Weather Contingency</h4>
   <ul>
    <li><i class="fas fa-sun fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Beautiful Day:</span> <span class="detail-value">Take full advantage by exploring all areas of the castle grounds. The white walls are particularly stunning against a blue sky. Don't miss the views from the top floor of the main keep.</span></li>
    <li><i class="fas fa-cloud-rain fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Rainy Day:</span> <span class="detail-value">The castle is still impressive in the rain, and crowds will be smaller. Focus on the covered passageways and interior spaces. The main keep provides shelter, and the misty atmosphere can create a different but equally beautiful experience.</span></li>
   </ul>

  <div class="details-content-wrapper">
    <h4>Activity Info</h4>

Features nine distinct walled gardens in various Edo period styles (e.g., pond garden, tea garden, flower garden). Very peaceful and photogenic.</span></li>

    <h4>Cultural Context</h4>
    <p>Kokoen was built in 1992 on the former site of the feudal lord's west residence (Nishi-Oyashiki). Despite being relatively new, the garden was meticulously designed following traditional Japanese garden principles from the Edo period (1603-1868). Each of the nine walled gardens represents a different style, showcasing various elements of Japanese garden design including:</p>
    
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Pond Garden (Kasui-en)</strong> - Featuring a central pond with islands and bridges</li>
      <li><strong>Tea Garden (Matsu-no-roji)</strong> - Designed to prepare visitors mentally for the tea ceremony</li>
      <li><strong>Bamboo Garden (Chikutei)</strong> - Showcasing various bamboo species</li>
      <li><strong>Flower Garden (Natsumono-niwa)</strong> - With seasonal blooms</li>
      <li><strong>Pine Tree Garden (Shoin-tei)</strong> - Featuring carefully pruned pine trees</li>
    </ul>
    
    <p>The garden also houses the Souju-an Tea House where you can experience a traditional Japanese tea ceremony (for an additional fee, typically around ¥500-700).</p>
    
    <h4>Seasonal Highlights</h4>
    <p>In May, you may see:</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Late azaleas and rhododendrons</li>
      <li>Early hydrangeas</li>
      <li>Irises in the pond areas</li>
      <li>Fresh green maple leaves</li>
    </ul>
    
    <h4>Weather Contingency</h4>
    <ul>
      <li><i class="fas fa-sun fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Beautiful Day:</span> <span class="detail-value">Take your time exploring all nine gardens. The sunlight filtering through trees creates beautiful patterns and highlights the water features.</span></li>
      <li><i class="fas fa-cloud-rain fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Rainy Day:</span> <span class="detail-value">Japanese gardens are often considered even more beautiful in light rain. The garden has covered walkways connecting some areas, and the Souju-an Tea House offers shelter while enjoying a traditional tea experience.</span></li>

      <h4>Cultural Context</h4>
      <p>Engyoji Temple was founded in 966 CE and is one of the most important temples of the Tendai sect of Buddhism. The temple complex consists of around 30 buildings spread across the forested mountain, creating a serene and spiritual atmosphere. Its remote mountain setting was designed to separate monks from worldly distractions during their training.</p>
      
      <p>The temple gained international fame when it was used as a filming location for "The Last Samurai" starring Tom Cruise. The main hall (Maniden) and other structures featured prominently in the film.</p>
      
      <p>Key buildings to visit include:</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Maniden (Main Hall)</strong> - The largest wooden building, standing on stilts on the mountainside</li>
        <li><strong>Daikodo (Great Lecture Hall)</strong> - Contains important Buddhist statues</li>
        <li><strong>Jikido (Dining Hall)</strong> - Where monks traditionally took their meals</li>
      </ul>
      
      <h4>Practical Tips</h4>
      <ul>
        <li>There's a significant amount of walking between temple buildings on the mountain</li>
        <li>Wear comfortable shoes suitable for forest paths</li>
        <li>Bring water, especially on warm days</li>
        <li>The last ropeway and bus times are critical - miss them and you'll need a taxi back</li>
      </ul>
      
      <h4>Weather Contingency</h4>
      <ul>
        <li><i class="fas fa-sun fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Beautiful Day:</span> <span class="detail-value">This is the perfect time to visit Mt. Shosha. The forest setting is magical in good weather, and you'll enjoy spectacular views of Himeji city from the mountain.</span></li>
        <li><i class="fas fa-cloud-rain fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Rainy Day:</span> <span class="detail-value">Consider skipping this option if it's raining heavily, as the paths between buildings can become slippery. The mist can create an atmospheric experience, but safety and comfort should be prioritized.</span></li>
      </ul>

<h3>Reflections on Himeji Castle</h3>

<p>After your visit to Himeji Castle, you might appreciate knowing that it's considered one of Japan's "Three Famous Castles" along with Matsumoto Castle and Kumamoto Castle. However, Himeji is widely regarded as the most impressive and complete of the three.</p>

<p>The castle complex is designed with a spiral layout that forces attackers to wind their way up through increasingly difficult defensive positions. This brilliant defensive design includes:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Narrow paths that prevent large groups from advancing together</li>
  <li>Over 80 buildings with intricate connecting passages</li>
  <li>Hidden defensive positions and "trick" gates</li>
  <li>Walls designed to allow defenders to fire arrows or drop stones on attackers</li>
  <li>Small windows and openings positioned at strategic angles</li>
</ul>

<p>Despite its formidable defenses, Himeji Castle was never attacked in battle. Its imposing presence alone was enough to deter potential invaders, serving as a powerful symbol of the feudal lord's authority.</p>

<h3>Accessibility Notes</h3>

<p>While exploring Himeji Castle and surroundings, keep in mind:</p>

<ul>
  <li>The main keep involves steep, narrow stairs and is not wheelchair accessible</li>
  <li>Kokoen Garden has some accessible paths but also includes steps and uneven surfaces</li>
  <li>The castle grounds outside the main structures are mostly accessible with assistance</li>
  <li>Mt. Shosha/Engyoji Temple involves significant walking on uneven terrain</li>
  <li>For those with mobility concerns, focusing on the castle exterior views and lower levels may be preferable</li>
</ul>
