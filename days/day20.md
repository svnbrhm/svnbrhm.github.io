<h3 id="atami-day">Discovering Atami's Art and Culture</h3>

<p>Today you'll explore the cultural side of Atami, balancing museum visits with local exploration. The morning takes you to the architecturally stunning MOA Museum of Art, home to an impressive collection of Japanese and East Asian treasures. In the afternoon, you'll discover Atami's charming town center with its retro shopping street, historic villa, and beachfront. Throughout the day, you'll still have plenty of time to enjoy the amenities of your resort.</p>

<p>Atami has been a popular hot spring destination for centuries, but it also has a rich cultural heritage that you'll experience today through both traditional and contemporary Japanese art and architecture.</p>

   <h4>About MOA Museum of Art</h4>
   <p>Founded by religious leader Mokichi Okada in 1982, the MOA Museum of Art houses an extraordinary collection of Japanese and East Asian art treasures. The museum itself is an architectural marvel, built into a hillside with spectacular views of Sagami Bay. Visitors enter through a dramatic 120-meter tunnel with moving walkways that leads into the mountain.</p>
   
   <h4>Collection Highlights</h4>
   <ul>
     <li><strong>National Treasures:</strong> The museum houses several designated National Treasures, including the "Red and White Plum Blossoms" folding screen by Ogata Kōrin</li>
     <li><strong>Japanese Art:</strong> Extensive collection of paintings, calligraphy, ceramics, and lacquerware spanning multiple periods</li>
     <li><strong>East Asian Art:</strong> Chinese ceramics and other artifacts</li>
     <li><strong>Tea Ceremony Items:</strong> Notable collection of tea ceremony utensils and related artifacts</li>
   </ul>
   
   <h4>Special Features</h4>
   <ul>
     <li><strong>Golden Tea Room:</strong> A recreation of Toyotomi Hideyoshi's famous gold-leafed tea room</li>
     <li><strong>Japanese Garden:</strong> Beautiful traditional garden with seasonal flowers</li>
     <li><strong>Noh Theater:</strong> Traditional Japanese theater space (check if performances are scheduled)</li>
     <li><strong>Panoramic Views:</strong> Several viewing spots offer stunning vistas of Sagami Bay</li>
   </ul>
   
   <h4>Weather Contingency</h4>
   <ul>
    <li><i class="fas fa-sun fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Beautiful Day:</span> <span class="detail-value">Take time to enjoy the outdoor gardens and panoramic viewing areas that showcase Atami's coastal scenery.</span></li>

   <h4>About Atami Ginza</h4>
   <p>Atami Ginza is a traditional shopping arcade that captures the nostalgic atmosphere of mid-20th century Japan. This covered shopping street is lined with small shops selling local specialties, souvenirs, and snacks. It offers a glimpse into the heyday of Atami as a popular hot spring resort town during Japan's economic boom period.</p>
   
   <h4>About Kiunkaku Villa</h4>
   <p>Built in 1919 as a luxurious second home for a wealthy businessman, Kiunkaku is a fascinating blend of traditional Japanese and Western architectural styles. The villa features tatami rooms alongside Western-style parlors, beautiful gardens with a tea house, and even an early example of a Western-style bathroom. It represents the cultural fusion that occurred during Japan's modernization in the early 20th century.</p>
   
   <h4>About Atami Sun Beach</h4>
   <p>This artificial beach along Atami's waterfront offers a pleasant seaside promenade with views of Sagami Bay. While not a swimming destination in May, it provides a refreshing coastal walk with mountain views in the background. The beach is illuminated in the evening, creating a romantic atmosphere.</p>

   <h4>About Japanese Kissaten Culture</h4>
   <p>Kissaten are traditional Japanese coffee houses that flourished from the post-war period through the 1980s. Unlike modern chain cafes, these establishments often feature carefully hand-dripped coffee, classic desserts like custard pudding or fruit sandwiches, and a quiet, contemplative atmosphere. Owners typically take great pride in their coffee preparation methods, which can be quite theatrical. Visiting a kissaten in Atami offers a glimpse into Japan's coffee culture before the arrival of international chains.</p>
