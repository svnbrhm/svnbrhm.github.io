   <h4>Castle History</h4>
   <p>Osaka Castle was originally built in 1583 by Toyotomi Hideyoshi, a powerful feudal lord who unified Japan. After his death, the castle was destroyed by Tokugawa forces in 1615, rebuilt by the Tokugawa shogunate, burned down in the 1660s, and reconstructed again. The current main tower is a concrete reconstruction from 1931, with major renovations completed in 1997.</p>
   
   <h4>Highlights</h4>
   <ul>
     <li><strong>Main Castle Tower:</strong> Eight floors of museum exhibits with historical artifacts, armor, and interactive displays. The observation deck on the top floor offers panoramic views of Osaka</li>
     <li><strong>Nishinomaru Garden:</strong> Beautiful garden featuring 600 cherry trees (though May is past peak bloom)</li>
     <li><strong>Stone Walls and Moats:</strong> Impressive defensive structures with massive stones, some weighing over 100 tons</li>
     <li><strong>Plum Grove:</strong> Garden area with 1,270 plum trees</li>
   </ul>
   
   <h4>Visiting Tips</h4>
   <ul>
     <li>Start with the castle grounds and work your way to the main tower</li>
     <li>The castle museum has English information and audio guides available</li>
     <li>There's an elevator in the castle tower for those who prefer not to use stairs</li>
     <li>Bring water, especially on warm days, as the park is quite large</li>
   </ul>
   
   <h4>Weather Contingency</h4>
   <ul>
    <li><i class="fas fa-sun fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Beautiful Day:</span> <span class="detail-value">Take your time exploring the expansive castle grounds and enjoy the views from the observation deck.</span></li>
    <li><i class="fas fa-cloud-rain fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Rainy Day:</span> <span class="detail-value">Focus on the interior museum exhibits in the castle tower. The covered walkways near the entrance provide some shelter.</span></li>

   <h4>History of Shinsekai</h4>
   <p>Shinsekai (meaning "New World") was developed in 1912 as an entertainment district with the northern half modeled after Paris and the southern half after Coney Island in New York. The area featured Luna Park, Japan's first amusement park, with Tsutenkaku Tower as its centerpiece. After World War II, the area fell into decline but has preserved much of its pre-war character, making it a fascinating window into Japan's past.</p>
   
   <h4>Must-See Spots</h4>
   <ul>
     <li><strong>Tsutenkaku Tower:</strong> The 103-meter tower with an observation deck offering views of the city. Don't miss the Billiken statue, Osaka's "God of Happiness" - rubbing his feet is said to bring good luck</li>
     <li><strong>Janjan Yokocho:</strong> Narrow alley lined with small restaurants and bars, preserving the atmosphere of old Osaka</li>
     <li><strong>Jan-Jan Town:</strong> Shopping arcade with retro game centers, fortune tellers, and small shops</li>
     <li><strong>Spa World:</strong> Massive public bath complex with themed baths from around the world (if you have extra time)</li>
   </ul>
   
   <h4>Cultural Note</h4>
   <p>Shinsekai has had a reputation for being rougher than other parts of Osaka, but it's perfectly safe for tourists during the day and evening. The area is especially known for its working-class atmosphere and unpretentious charm - a stark contrast to the polished shopping districts elsewhere in the city.</p>
   
   <h4>Weather Contingency</h4>
   <ul>
    <li><i class="fas fa-sun fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Beautiful Day:</span> <span class="detail-value">Definitely visit the Tsutenkaku observation deck for panoramic views of Osaka.</span></li>

<h4>Weather in Osaka (May)</h4>
<p>May in Osaka is generally pleasant with mild temperatures:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Average high: 24°C (75°F)</li>
  <li>Average low: 16°C (61°F)</li>
  <li>Occasional rain showers possible</li>
  <li>For outdoor activities, check the forecast and consider bringing a small umbrella</li>
</ul>

<h4>What to Bring for Either Option</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Comfortable shoes:</strong> Both the Expo and Castle/Shinsekai options involve significant walking</li>
  <li><strong>IC Card:</strong> Keep your ICOCA, PASMO, or Suica card charged for convenient transit</li>
  <li><strong>Cash:</strong> While cards are increasingly accepted, some smaller shops in Shinsekai may be cash-only</li>
  <li><strong>Power bank:</strong> Useful for keeping your phone charged for navigation and photos</li>
  <li><strong>Light jacket or layer:</strong> Evenings can be cooler, especially if attending TeamLab</li>
  <li><strong>Water bottle:</strong> Stay hydrated during your adventures</li>
</ul>

<h4>Choosing Between Options</h4>
<p>Consider these factors when deciding between Option A (Expo) and Option B (Castle & Shinsekai):</p>

<h5>Choose Option A (Expo) if you:</h5>
<ul>
  <li>Are interested in cutting-edge technology and future-focused exhibits</li>
  <li>Enjoy large international events and exhibitions</li>
  <li>Want to experience a once-every-five-years global event</li>
  <li>Are comfortable with potentially larger crowds and more structured environments</li>
</ul>

<h5>Choose Option B (Castle & Shinsekai) if you:</h5>
<ul>
  <li>Prefer historical sites and authentic local neighborhoods</li>
  <li>Want to experience both traditional and retro aspects of Osaka</li>
  <li>Enjoy more flexible, self-paced exploration</li>
  <li>Are interested in unique local food experiences like kushikatsu</li>
</ul>

<h4>Language Tips</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Expo 2025:</strong> Will have significant English signage and likely English-speaking staff</li>
  <li><strong>Osaka Castle:</strong> Has English information and audio guides available</li>
  <li><strong>Shinsekai:</strong> Less English support, but many restaurants have picture menus or food displays</li>
  <li>Useful phrases:
    <ul>
      <li>"Sumimasen" (Excuse me/I'm sorry) - useful for getting attention</li>
      <li>"Eigo no menu arimasu ka?" (Do you have an English menu?)</li>
      <li>"Kore o kudasai" (This please) - useful when pointing at menu items</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
</ul>
