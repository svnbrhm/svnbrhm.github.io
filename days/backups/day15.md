<h3 id="osaka-intro">Welcome to Osaka</h3>

<p>Osaka is Japan's vibrant third-largest city, known for its outgoing locals, amazing street food, and energetic nightlife. Unlike Tokyo's polish or Kyoto's tradition, Osaka embraces a more down-to-earth character, where merchants and food have historically taken center stage. The local saying "Osaka wa kuidaore" (eat until you drop in Osaka) perfectly captures the city's food-loving spirit.</p>

<p>Today you'll travel from the historic castle town of Himeji to the bustling urban center of Osaka, settling into the lively Namba district. This area is home to the famous Dotonbori entertainment district with its iconic neon signs, the shopping paradise of Shinsaibashi, and the youth fashion hub of Amerikamura. Get ready for sensory overload in the best possible way!</p>

   <h4>Navigating Osaka/Umeda Stations</h4>
   <p>Osaka/Umeda is one of Japan's largest and most complex station areas. Some tips:</p>
   <ul>
    <li>JR Osaka Station and Subway Umeda Station are connected but require a 5-7 minute walk through underground passages</li>
    <li>Follow the red "M" signs for Midosuji Line</li>
    <li>Use your IC card (PASMO, SUICA, ICOCA) for seamless transfers</li>
    <li>If confused, station staff can assist - simply show them "難波駅" (Namba Station) on your phone</li>
   </ul>
   
   <h4>Navigating Namba Station Area</h4>
   <p>Namba is another large station complex with multiple railway companies. When you arrive:</p>
   <ul>
    <li>Exit toward "South" or "Nankai" for the most direct route to Dotonbori area</li>
    <li>Look for signs to "Namba Walk" - a large underground shopping mall connecting different parts of the station</li>
    <li>The Dotonbori canal area is approximately a 5-7 minute walk north from Namba Station</li>
   </ul>
   
   <h4>Weather Contingency</h4>
   <ul>
    <li><i class="fas fa-cloud-rain fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Rainy Day:</span> <span class="detail-value">Osaka's extensive underground walkway network allows you to navigate much of the city without going outside. Follow signs for "Namba Walk" to stay dry.</span></li>

<h3 id="practical-info">Practical Information for Osaka</h3>

<h4>Transportation in Osaka</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Subway System:</strong> Osaka has an extensive subway network operated by two companies (Osaka Metro and Hanshin). The most useful lines for tourists are:
    <ul>
      <li>Midosuji Line (Red) - North-south backbone connecting Umeda and Namba</li>
      <li>Sennichimae Line (Pink) - East-west line through Namba</li>
      <li>Yotsubashi Line (Blue) - Serves Namba and Shinsaibashi</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
  <li><strong>IC Cards:</strong> Your PASMO, Suica, ICOCA or other IC cards work on all public transportation in Osaka</li>
  <li><strong>Taxis:</strong> Readily available, especially in Namba and Dotonbori areas. Flag fall is approximately ¥680</li>
</ul>

<h4>Useful Japanese Phrases</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Sumimasen</strong> - Excuse me/I'm sorry (useful for getting attention)</li>
  <li><strong>Kore o kudasai</strong> - This please (while pointing)</li>
  <li><strong>Ikura desu ka?</strong> - How much is it?</li>
  <li><strong>Eigo no menu arimasu ka?</strong> - Do you have an English menu?</li>
  <li><strong>Osusume wa nan desu ka?</strong> - What do you recommend?</li>
  <li><strong>Oishii desu</strong> - It's delicious</li>
  <li><strong>Arigatō gozaimasu</strong> - Thank you very much</li>
</ul>

<h4>Money & Payment</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Many places in Dotonbori and street food stalls still prefer cash</li>
  <li>ATMs are available at 7-Eleven, post offices, and major banks</li>
  <li>Credit cards are increasingly accepted at larger restaurants and shops</li>
  <li>IC cards can be used for small purchases at convenience stores and some vending machines</li>
</ul>

<h4>Weather in May</h4>
<p>Osaka in May typically experiences pleasant spring weather:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Average temperatures: 16-24°C (61-75°F)</li>
  <li>Occasional rain showers, so carrying a small umbrella is wise</li>
  <li>Evenings can be cooler, so a light jacket is recommended</li>
</ul>

<h4>Connectivity</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Free WiFi is available at Namba Station, major shopping centers, and many cafes</li>
  <li>Your hotel provides free WiFi</li>
  <li>Consider having offline maps downloaded for navigation</li>
</ul>

<h4>Osaka vs. Tokyo: Cultural Differences</h4>
<p>You might notice some differences between Osaka and Tokyo:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Osakans are known for being more outgoing and direct than Tokyoites</li>
  <li>People stand on the right side of escalators (opposite of Tokyo)</li>
  <li>Osaka dialect (Kansai-ben) has distinct expressions and intonation</li>
  <li>Food culture is central to Osaka's identity - the city is known as "Japan's kitchen"</li>
</ul>
