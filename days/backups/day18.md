<h3 id="higashiyama-intro">Kyoto's Historic Eastern Hills</h3>

<p>Today you'll explore Kyoto's most atmospheric and historically significant districts - the preserved streets of Higashiyama and the geiko (Kyoto's term for geisha) district of Gion. This area showcases traditional Kyoto at its most picturesque, with narrow lanes lined by wooden machiya townhouses, historic temples, and traditional shops that have operated for generations.</p>

<p>The walking route through these eastern hills offers iconic views of Kyoto and some of Japan's most photographed scenes. While Sunday crowds will be considerable, the beauty and cultural significance of this area make it an essential Kyoto experience.</p>

### Kyoto: Higashiyama & Gion Exploration

*Focus: Exploring iconic Higashiyama & Gion, using subway access from Nijo, Stüssy stop, diverse food.*

   <h4>Key Highlights Along the Route</h4>
   
   <h5>Yasaka Shrine</h5>
   <ul>
     <li>One of Kyoto's most important Shinto shrines, dating from the 7th century</li>
     <li>Features a main hall (honden) and worship hall (haiden) connected by a central corridor</li>
     <li>Known for its large stone torii gate at the entrance</li>
     <li>Free to enter the main grounds</li>
   </ul>
   
   <h5>Sannenzaka & Ninenzaka (Preserved Slopes)</h5>
   <ul>
     <li>Historic pedestrian walkways paved with stone steps</li>
     <li>Lined with traditional wooden buildings housing shops and teahouses</li>
     <li>Local superstition says falling on Ninenzaka ("Two-Year Slope") brings two years of bad luck</li>
     <li>Shops sell traditional crafts, sweets, ceramics, and souvenirs</li>
   </ul>
   
   <h5>Kiyomizu-dera Temple</h5>
   <ul>
     <li>UNESCO World Heritage Site founded in 778 CE</li>
     <li>Famous for its wooden stage that extends 13 meters over the hillside</li>
     <li>The main hall was built without using a single nail</li>
     <li>Otowa Waterfall at the base offers visitors sacred water believed to grant wishes</li>
     <li>Offers spectacular views over Kyoto, especially beautiful in autumn</li>
   </ul>
   
   <h5>Hanamikoji Street & Gion</h5>
   <ul>
     <li>Traditional entertainment district where geiko (Kyoto's geisha) and maiko (apprentice geiko) perform</li>
     <li>Preserved wooden machiya townhouses line the streets</li>
     <li>Many exclusive ochaya (tea houses) where geiko entertain guests</li>
     <li>Best time to potentially spot geiko/maiko is around dusk (5:30-6:00 PM) as they head to appointments</li>
   </ul>
   
   <h4>Gion Etiquette</h4>
   <p>Please respect the following guidelines when visiting Gion:</p>
   <ul>
     <li>Do not touch or stop geiko/maiko or block their path</li>
     <li>Ask permission before taking photos of performers</li>
     <li>Avoid entering private properties or peering through windows</li>
     <li>Keep noise levels down in this residential area</li>
     <li>Some streets in Gion now prohibit photography - observe posted signs</li>
   </ul>
   
   <h4>Weather Contingency</h4>
   <ul>
    <li><i class="fas fa-sun fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Beautiful Day:</span> <span class="detail-value">Take your time at Kiyomizu-dera's wooden stage for panoramic views of Kyoto. The walk along Ninenzaka/Sannenzaka is particularly beautiful in good weather.</span></li>
    <li><i class="fas fa-cloud-rain fa-fw"></i> <span class="detail-label">Rainy Day:</span> <span class="detail-value">Many shops along Ninenzaka/Sannenzaka have covered entrances.      <h4>About Kuzukiri</h4>
     <p>Kuzukiri is a traditional Japanese sweet consisting of transparent noodles made from kuzu (Japanese arrowroot) starch. The noodles have a unique texture - firm yet slippery - and are served chilled with a sweet black sugar syrup for dipping. Kagizen Yoshifusa has been making this specialty for over 300 years and is considered one of the finest places in Kyoto to experience traditional Japanese confectionery.</p>

<h3 id="practical-info">Practical Information for Higashiyama & Gion</h3>

<h4>Photography in Gion</h4>
<p>Please note that since 2019, photography restrictions have been implemented in parts of Gion due to inappropriate tourist behavior:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Photography is prohibited on private streets in the Gion district, with fines up to ¥10,000</li>
  <li>Never photograph geiko or maiko without explicit permission</li>
  <li>Look for "No Photography" signs and respect them</li>
  <li>Public streets and major attractions remain photography-friendly</li>
</ul>

<h4>Crowd Management on Sundays</h4>
<p>Sunday is one of the busiest days in Kyoto's popular areas. To make your experience more enjoyable:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Visit Kiyomizu-dera earlier in the day if possible</li>
  <li>Explore side streets and less-known paths to avoid the worst crowds</li>
  <li>Be patient and allow extra time for popular spots</li>
  <li>Consider visiting Yasaka Shrine later in the afternoon when crowds thin out</li>
</ul>

<h4>Footwear Advice</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Wear comfortable walking shoes - today involves significant walking on hills and stone pavements</li>
  <li>The stone paths of Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka can be slippery when wet</li>
  <li>Many temples require removing shoes before entering certain buildings</li>
</ul>

<h4>Cultural Etiquette at Temples and Shrines</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Temples (Buddhist):</strong> Remove hats, speak quietly, and follow signs about photography</li>
  <li><strong>Shrines (Shinto):</strong> Bow slightly before entering through torii gates, wash hands at purification fountains</li>
  <li><strong>Prayer Rituals:</strong> At shrines - bow twice, clap twice, bow once more</li>
  <li><strong>Respect:</strong> Avoid touching religious artifacts or stepping on thresholds</li>
</ul>

<h4>Weather Considerations in May</h4>
<ul>
  <li>May weather in Kyoto is generally pleasant but can be unpredictable</li>
  <li>Temperatures typically range from 15-25°C (59-77°F)</li>
  <li>Sudden rain showers are possible - consider bringing a small umbrella</li>
  <li>Sun protection is recommended, especially when walking in open areas</li>
</ul>
