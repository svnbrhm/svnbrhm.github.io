     <h4>Japanese Café Context</h4>
     <p>Kissaten (喫茶店) are traditional Japanese coffee shops that first appeared in the early 20th century. Unlike modern chain cafes, they often feature meticulous hand-dripped coffee preparation, dark wood interiors, and a quiet, contemplative atmosphere. Many historic kissaten in Ginza maintain techniques and recipes unchanged for decades, making them cultural treasures in Tokyo's coffee scene.</p>
